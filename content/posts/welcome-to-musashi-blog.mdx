Welcome to **Musashi Blog** — a place where we sharpen workflows to perfection.

The Japanese swordsman Miyamoto Musashi wrote in *The Book of Five Rings*:

> "Do nothing that is of no use."

That philosophy is the foundation of this blog. Every automation we build, every workflow we design, every tool we evaluate — it must have purpose. No wasted motion. No redundant steps.

## What you will find here

This blog is dedicated to the craft of automation. Specifically:

- Deep dives into **n8n** — the self-hostable, open-source automation platform
- Practical tutorials on **Make.com** (formerly Integromat)
- Real-world automation case studies from actual workflows
- Tool reviews and comparisons between automation platforms
- Tips for connecting APIs without writing endless boilerplate code

## Why automation?

The modern knowledge worker drowns in repetitive tasks. Data entry. Report generation. Notification routing. File management. Each one small, but together they consume hours every week — hours that could be spent on actual thinking.

Automation is not about being lazy. It is about directing your energy where it matters most.

Musashi trained with relentless precision so that in the moment of action, no energy was wasted on technique — it was all reserved for intent.

That is what good automation does for your workflow.
